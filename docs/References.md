# References

This project combines concepts from neuroradiology, neuroanatomy, neuropathology, and deep learning for MRI-based brain tumor classification. The following references provide the scientific foundation for the educational content presented in this repository.

---

# Neuroradiology

### Osborn's Brain

Osborn AG, Hedlund GL, Salzman KL.

**Osborn's Brain: Imaging, Pathology, and Anatomy.**

2nd Edition.

Elsevier.

One of the most comprehensive references for brain MRI interpretation, covering normal anatomy, tumor imaging, and differential diagnosis.

---

### Diagnostic Imaging: Brain

Osborn AG, Salzman KL, Barkovich AJ.

**Diagnostic Imaging: Brain.**

Elsevier.

A practical neuroradiology reference widely used by radiologists for interpreting intracranial lesions.

---

### MRI Made Easy

Govind B. Chavhan.

**MRI Made Easy (for Beginners).**

Jaypee Brothers Medical Publishers.

Provides an accessible introduction to MRI physics and sequence interpretation.

---

# Neuroanatomy

### Gray's Anatomy

Standring S. (Editor)

**Gray's Anatomy: The Anatomical Basis of Clinical Practice.**

41st Edition.

Elsevier.

Authoritative reference for human anatomy, including detailed brain and meningeal anatomy.

---

### Neuroanatomy Through Clinical Cases

Hal Blumenfeld.

**Neuroanatomy Through Clinical Cases.**

Oxford University Press.

Excellent resource linking neuroanatomy with neurological symptoms and imaging findings.

---

# Neuropathology

### WHO Classification of Central Nervous System Tumors

World Health Organization.

**WHO Classification of Tumours of the Central Nervous System.**

5th Edition.

International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC).

The international standard for the classification and diagnosis of CNS tumors.

---

### Greenberg's Handbook of Neurosurgery

Greenberg MS.

**Handbook of Neurosurgery.**

A widely used neurosurgical reference covering diagnosis, pathology, and treatment of brain tumors.

---

# MRI Sequences

### MRI in Practice

Catherine Westbrook, John Talbot.

**MRI in Practice.**

5th Edition.

Wiley-Blackwell.

A standard reference explaining MRI physics and clinical MRI sequences, including T1, T2, FLAIR, DWI, ADC, and contrast-enhanced imaging.

---

### Handbook of MRI Technique

Catherine Westbrook.

**Handbook of MRI Technique.**

Comprehensive guide to MRI acquisition, sequence selection, and image interpretation.

---

# Deep Learning and Medical Imaging

### Deep Learning

Goodfellow I, Bengio Y, Courville A.

**Deep Learning.**

MIT Press.

Foundational textbook covering neural networks, convolutional neural networks (CNNs), optimization, and modern deep learning methods.

---

### Deep Learning for Medical Image Analysis

Zhou SK, Greenspan H, Shen D. (Editors)

**Deep Learning for Medical Image Analysis.**

Academic Press.

Focuses on CNN-based analysis of radiological images, segmentation, detection, and classification.

---

# Brain Tumor MRI Datasets

### Figshare Brain Tumor Dataset

Cheng J.

**Brain Tumor Dataset.**

A publicly available MRI dataset commonly used for research on glioma, meningioma, and pituitary tumor classification.

---

### Brain Tumor Segmentation (BraTS) Challenge

Menze BH, Bakas S, et al.

**The Multimodal Brain Tumor Image Segmentation Benchmark (BraTS).**

An internationally recognized benchmark dataset for brain tumor segmentation and MRI analysis.

---

# Clinical Guidelines

### NCCN Guidelines

National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN).

**Central Nervous System Cancers Guidelines.**

Evidence-based recommendations for diagnosis and management of CNS tumors.

---

### European Association of Neuro-Oncology (EANO)

**EANO Guidelines for Adult Diffuse Gliomas, Meningiomas, and Pituitary Tumors.**

Current clinical recommendations for diagnosis, imaging, and treatment.

---

# Notes

The educational content in this repository is intended to summarize established concepts from the above references for learning purposes. It should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

Whenever possible, readers are encouraged to consult the original textbooks, peer-reviewed publications, and clinical guidelines for more detailed information.
