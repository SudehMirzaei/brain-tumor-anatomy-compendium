These figures provide anatomical context for understanding the origin, growth pattern, and MRI appearance of common brain tumors.

## Purpose

These anatomical illustrations are intended to complement the MRI examples presented in the Glioma, Meningioma, and Pituitary Tumor sections.

Understanding normal neuroanatomy is essential for recognizing:

- Intra-axial versus extra-axial tumors
- Sellar and suprasellar anatomy
- Corpus callosum involvement
- Optic chiasm compression
- Cavernous sinus invasion
- Ventricular compression and mass effect
